Disability in Latin America: Rights, Inclusion, and Digital Accessibility for All

The history of disability in Latin America is a complex narrative marked by challenges, but also by significant progress in the fight for human rights and social inclusion.
Throughout history, people with disabilities have faced significant barriers to fully participating in society due to discrimination, stigma, and lack of accessibility.

Minister Macaé Evaristo Reaffirms Brazil's Commitment to the Human Rights Violations Reparations and Victim Care Agenda

The event brought together public officials, civil society representatives, and movements of families and victims of human rights violations to discuss the implementation of decisions by the Inter-American Court of Human Rights and the consolidation of memory, truth, and reparation policies in the country.

Government Organizes 4th State LGBTQIA+ Conference and Promotes Various Activities during Pride Month

With the theme “Building the State Policy for the Rights of LGBTQIA+ People,” the conference will propose guidelines for the creation and implementation of public policies. The gathering of these proposals will contribute to the State Plan for the Promotion of Human Rights and Citizenship of LGBTQIA+ People.

News by Gov.br - Rio Grande do Sul

15th Cinema and Human Rights Exhibition Discusses Climate Emergency with Productions by Indigenous, Quilombola, and Riverside Filmmakers

With the theme “Human Rights and Climate Emergency: Towards a Sustainable Future,” the 15th Cinema and Human Rights Exhibition aligns with the defense of environmental justice, focusing on productions by indigenous, quilombola, and riverside filmmakers. This edition will be held in 12 capitals across Brazil from November 26 to December 10, 2025, with free screenings and open debates.
