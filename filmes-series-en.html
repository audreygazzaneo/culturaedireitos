MOVIES & SERIES

Movies

Carandiru: The Movie

Release Date: April 11, 2003

The film directed by Héctor Babenco shows human rights violations in the Brazilian prison system. Based on the experiences of doctor Dráuzio Varella, it portrays overcrowding, lack of dignity, police violence, and the Carandiru Massacre, where 111 inmates were killed.
The work denounces the dehumanization of prisoners, social inequality, and state abandonment, showing that even prisoners have the right to life, respect, and dignified treatment. The film invites reflection on the true role of prisons: to punish or rehabilitate.


Rating: R (Restricted) - 17+

Available on streaming platforms: Netflix, GloboPlay, Amazon Prime Video,
YouTube, Google Play, and Apple TV.

Hotel Rwanda

Release Date: August 19, 2005

The film portrays the Rwandan genocide in 1994, when thousands of Tutsis were killed by the Hutus. Based on real events, it tells the story of manager Paul Rusesabagina, who shelters refugees in a hotel to save them from death.
The work addresses serious human rights violations, such as the right to life, racial equality, and the omission of the international community in the face of the tragedy. At the same time, it highlights solidarity and courage of those who defend human dignity amidst violence.


Rating: PG-13 (Parents Strongly Cautioned) - 13+

Available on streaming platforms: Amazon Prime Video

The Trial of the Chicago 7

Release Date: September 25, 2020

The film shows the trial of activists who protested against the Vietnam War and were unjustly accused of inciting violence. The work addresses human rights violations such as lack of a fair trial, racism, and denial of freedom of speech and protest. The film defends the importance of justice, equality, and freedom in a democratic society.


Rating: R (Restricted) - 17+

Available on streaming platforms: Netflix

TV Series

Maid

Release Date: October 1, 2021

"Maid" follows Alex, a mother who escapes an abusive relationship and struggles to survive as a domestic worker. The series addresses issues of domestic violence, social inequality, and the difficulty in accessing basic rights, highlighting the fight for dignity and autonomy in the face of oppressive systems.


Rating: TV-MA (Mature Audiences) - For viewers aged 17 and older.

Available on streaming platforms: Netflix

Self Made

Release Date: March 20, 2020

The series tells the story of Madam C.J. Walker, who overcame racism and sexism to become a successful entrepreneur. It addresses human rights such as racial and gender equality, the right to work, and dignity, showing the importance of autonomy, respect, and equal opportunities for all.


Rating: TV-MA (Mature Audiences) - 17+

Available on streaming platforms: Netflix

Better Call Saul

Release Date: February 8, 2015

The series shows the life of lawyer Jimmy McGill, who faces injustices and ethical dilemmas in the legal system. It addresses topics related to human rights such as the right to defense, social inequality, abuse of power, and lack of true justice. The work highlights the importance of ethics, dignity, and equality within the law and society.


Rating: TV-MA (Mature Audiences) - 17+

Available on streaming platforms: Netflix
